Tortang Talong Recipe

Introduction:

Tortang Talong is a Filipino classic that's often hailed as one of the most delicious egg dishes around, according to Taste Atlas. This scrumptious eggplant omelette is simple yet incredibly satisfying. The eggplants are grilled or boiled until tender, then coated in a seasoned egg mixture and fried until golden brown. Whether you're a fan of egg dishes or just love trying new flavors, Tortang Talong is definitely a must-try. Its combination of smoky eggplant and fluffy egg is pure comfort food bliss!

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggplants
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil (vegetable or canola)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon onion powder (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Eggplants:
    • Grilling Method: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Place the eggplants directly on the grill grates and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft.
    • Boiling Method: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Carefully add the eggplants and cook for about 10-15 minutes, or until tender. Remove and let them cool.
  2. Peel and Prepare the Eggplants: After grilling or boiling, let the eggplants cool enough to handle. Peel off the skin. The flesh should be soft and mushy. Flatten each eggplant by gently pressing it with a fork or the back of a spoon.
  3. Prepare the Egg Mixture: In a bowl, beat the eggs. Season with salt, pepper, and optional garlic powder and onion powder. If using onions and tomatoes, mix them into the beaten eggs.
  4. Dipping and Cooking:
    • Heat the cooking oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat.
    • Dip each flattened eggplant into the egg mixture, ensuring it is well-coated.
    • Place the coated eggplant into the hot skillet. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the egg is golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Serving: Remove the cooked Tortang Talong from the skillet and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Serve hot with steamed rice and a dipping sauce made from vinegar, minced garlic, and a pinch of salt.

Tips:

  • For a heartier version: Add a filling of ground meat (pork, beef, or chicken) mixed with onions, tomatoes, and seasoning before dipping the eggplant in the egg mixture.
  • Dipping Sauce: You can also serve Tortang Talong with a side of soy sauce or a tomato-based dipping sauce for extra flavor.
  • For extra crispiness: Let the coated eggplant sit for a few minutes before frying to allow the egg to set and create a crispier texture.

Enjoy your delicious Tortang Talong, a comforting and flavorful Filipino classic!
