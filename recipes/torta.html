Tortang Talong Recipe

Introduction:

Tortang Talong is a Filipino classic that's often hailed as one of the most delicious egg dishes around, according to Taste Atlas. This scrumptious eggplant omelette is simple yet incredibly satisfying. The eggplants are grilled or boiled until tender, then coated in a seasoned egg mixture and fried until golden brown. Whether you're a fan of egg dishes or just love trying new flavors, Tortang Talong is definitely a must-try. Its combination of smoky eggplant and fluffy egg is pure comfort food bliss!

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggplants
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil (vegetable or canola)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh cilantro (optional, for garnish)
  • Tomato ketchup or hot sauce (for serving, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Eggplants: Grill or boil the eggplants until they are tender and the skin is charred (if grilling) or soft (if boiling). Let them cool slightly, then peel off the skin and mash the flesh with a fork.
  2. Prepare the Egg Mixture: In a bowl, beat the eggs and season with salt and pepper. You can also add finely chopped onion and tomato if you like.
  3. Combine: Mix the mashed eggplant into the egg mixture until well combined.
  4. Cook: Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Pour a portion of the eggplant-egg mixture into the skillet and spread it out to form a round omelette. Cook until the bottom is golden brown, then flip and cook the other side. Repeat with the remaining mixture.
  5. Serve: Serve the Tortang Talong hot with rice, or as a standalone dish with a side of ketchup or hot sauce.

Tips:

  • Grilling Eggplants: For extra flavor, grill the eggplants until the skin is charred. This gives a nice smoky taste to the dish.
  • Adding Ingredients: Feel free to add other ingredients like ground meat or cheese to the egg mixture for a different twist.
  • Cooking Heat: Make sure the skillet is properly heated before adding the egg mixture to prevent sticking.
  • Serving Ideas: Tortang Talong can be served with a side of garlic rice and a fresh tomato salad for a complete meal.
  • Storage: Leftovers can be stored in the fridge and reheated for a quick meal.

Tortang Talong is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed any time of the day. Enjoy your meal!
