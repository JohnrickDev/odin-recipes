Adobo Recipe

Introduction:

Adobo is one of the most iconic dishes in Filipino cuisine and for good reason. It's a savory, tangy, and slightly sweet stew that's packed with flavor. Made with chicken, pork, or even both, it's simmered in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and bay leaves until it's melt-in-your-mouth tender. Adobo is incredibly versatile and has earned its spot as a favorite comfort food in the Philippines and beyond.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken pieces (legs or thighs work best)
  • 1 lb pork belly, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (white or apple cider)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3-4 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Meat: In a large bowl, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and bay leaves. Add the chicken and pork pieces, making sure they're well-coated. Cover and let it marinate in the fridge for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for more flavor).
  2. Cook the Meat: Heat the cooking oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Remove the meat from the marinade and cook it in the pot until it's browned on all sides. You might need to do this in batches to avoid overcrowding the pot.
  3. Simmer: Add the chopped onion to the pot and cook until softened. Pour in the remaining marinade and water. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened. If you like a slightly sweet adobo, add the brown sugar to the pot.
  4. Season: Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
  5. Serve: Serve the adobo hot with steamed rice. Enjoy!

Tips:

  • Balance the flavors: Adjust the amount of soy sauce and vinegar to suit your taste. Some like it more tangy, while others prefer it saltier.
  • Use coconut milk: For a creamy variation, add a can of coconut milk to the pot during the simmering stage.
  • Add potatoes: Adding cubed potatoes can make the dish more hearty and absorb the delicious sauce.
  • Make ahead: Adobo tastes even better the next day, so feel free to make it ahead of time and store it in the fridge.
  • Experiment with spices: Some variations include adding whole peppercorns, bay leaves, or even a touch of chili for a bit of heat.

Adobo is a dish that gets even better the next day, so don't be surprised if you find yourself craving leftovers!
