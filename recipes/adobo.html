Adobo

Adobo is considered the national dish of the Philippines. It is a delicious, savory chicken dish with a garlic-y, soy sauce-based flavor, traditionally served with white rice for the whole family. It is the country's pride. With more than 7,000 islands, variations of Adobo can be found throughout the Philippines. Today, we'll be preparing the most popular and traditional Adobo recipe. Let's get started!


Ingredients

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) chicken thighs or pork shoulder, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar (white or apple cider)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 head garlic, peeled and crushed
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional)
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and quartered (optional)

Ingredients

  1. 1 kg (2.2 lbs) chicken thighs or pork shoulder, cut into pieces
  2. 1/2 cup soy sauce
  3. 1/4 cup vinegar (white or apple cider)
  4. 1/2 cup water
  5. 1 head garlic, peeled and crushed
  6. 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  7. 3 bay leaves
  8. 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  9. 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional)
  10. 2 medium potatoes, peeled and quartered (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken or pork pieces, soy sauce, and garlic. Marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight for better flavor.
  2. Heat the cooking oil in a large skillet or pot over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the marinated meat (reserve the marinade) to the skillet and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Pour in the reserved marinade, water, and vinegar. Do not stir.
  5. Add the black peppercorns and bay leaves. Bring to a boil.
  6. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let it simmer for about 30 to 40 minutes, or until the meat is tender.
  7. For a sweeter taste, add the brown sugar and stir to combine.
  8. Optional: Add the potatoes during the last 15 minutes of cooking and cook until they are tender.
  9. Remove the lid and simmer for an additional 5 to 10 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
  10. Serve the Adobo hot with steamed rice.
